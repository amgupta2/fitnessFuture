## Getting Started

### First Login

When you first sign in, you'll see:
- **Dashboard** - Your home base (empty at first)
- **Workouts** - Where you create templates
- **AI Coach** - Your virtual training partner
- **Analytics** - Your progress (needs data first)
- **Settings** - Your profile and preferences

**⚠️ Important:** Set up your profile in **Settings** first! Your experience level affects AI recommendations and auto-progression.

### Setting Your Experience Level

**Go to:** Settings → Training Profile

**Choose one:**
- **Beginner** (< 1 year) - You're learning form and building base strength
- **Intermediate** (1-3 years) - You can handle more volume and intensity
- **Advanced** (3+ years) - You need specialized programming

**Why it matters:** The app adjusts workout suggestions, progression speed, and recovery recommendations based on your level.

## Creating Your First Template

Templates are reusable workout plans like "Push Day" or "Leg Day."

### Step-by-Step

**1. Go to Workouts**
- Click the "+" button in the top right

**2. Name Your Template**
- Example: "Push Day A"
- Add a description (optional): "Chest, shoulders, triceps"
- Select category: Upper Body, Lower Body, Full Body, etc.

**3. Add Exercises**
- Click "Add Exercise"
- Search for an exercise (we have 471 in our library!)
- Or type a custom exercise name
- Set target sets, reps, and rest time

**Tips:**
- **Popular exercises** appear first when searching
- **Recent exercises** show exercises you've used before
- Click the **info icon** to see exercise images and details
- Use the **grip handle** to drag exercises and reorder them

### Exercise Settings Explained

| Setting | What It Means | Example |
|---------|---------------|---------|
| **Sets** | How many times you'll do this exercise | 3 |
| **Reps (Min-Max)** | Your target rep range | 8-12 |
| **Rest (seconds)** | How long to rest between sets | 90s |
| **Notes** | Reminders for yourself | "Focus on squeeze at top" |

### Smart Defaults

The app automatically suggests:
- **Compound movements** → 90-120s rest
- **Isolation exercises** → 60-90s rest
- **3 sets** for most exercises
- **8-12 reps** for hypertrophy

**You can change these anytime!**

### Editing & Organizing

- **Edit exercise:** Click the pencil icon
- **Delete exercise:** Click the trash icon (with confirmation)
- **Reorder:** Grab the grip handle (☰) and drag
- **Duplicate template:** Click "Duplicate" to make a copy

## Starting & Logging Workouts

### Starting a Session

**Go to:** Dashboard or Workouts → Click template → "Start Workout"

You'll see:
- Exercise navigation (swipe between exercises)
- Current exercise name
- Set counter (e.g., "Set 1 of 3")
- Input fields for weight and reps

### Logging Your Sets

**1. Enter Weight & Reps**
- Type the weight you lifted
- Type how many reps you completed

**2. Mark as Warmup (Optional)**
- Toggle "Warmup" for warm-up sets
- Warmup sets don't count toward progress

**3. Log Set**
- Click "Log Set"
- Rest timer starts automatically

**🎉 PR Celebration!**
If you set a personal record, you'll see:
- Fireworks animation
- "NEW PR!" banner
- Gold border on that set

### Rest Timer

**Automatic:** Starts after logging each set

**Features:**
- Big countdown display
- Skip button (top right)
- Progress bar showing time remaining
- Stays visible while you scroll

**Tip:** The timer uses your template's rest time, but you can skip it anytime if you're ready to go.

### Completing Your Workout

**Click "Finish"** when done

The app saves:
- All your sets (weight × reps)
- Total volume (weight × reps × sets)
- Any PRs you hit
- Timestamp for analytics

**⚠️ Caveat:** Can't edit sets after finishing. Log accurately!

## Understanding AI Coach

Your AI Coach has two modes: **Programmer** and **Trainer**

**Go to:** AI Coach (in main menu)

### Programmer Mode (Template Generation)

**Use it when:** You want a complete workout program

**How it works:**
1. Describe your goal: "I want a 4-day upper/lower split for hypertrophy"
2. The AI asks clarifying questions if needed
3. It generates complete templates with exercises, sets, reps, rest
4. Templates are saved directly to your account

**Example prompts:**
- "Create a 3-day full body routine for a beginner"
- "I need a push/pull/legs split, 6 days a week, for muscle growth"
- "Give me a leg day focused on quads, I have 60 minutes"

**What you get:**
- Professionally designed templates
- Exercises matched to your equipment
- Progressive overload built in
- Rest times based on exercise type

**💡 Pro Tip:** Be specific about:
- Days per week
- Equipment available
- Time per session
- Primary goal (strength/hypertrophy/endurance)

### Trainer Mode (Technique & Advice)

**Use it when:** You have a specific training question

**How it works:**
1. Ask a question naturally
2. The AI analyzes your training history
3. It provides evidence-based advice with cues

**Example questions:**
- "Why is my bench press stalling?"
- "What muscles does the Romanian deadlift work?"
- "How do I fix my squat depth?"
- "Should I train abs every day?"

**What you get:**
- Target muscle breakdowns
- Technique cues ("drive through heels")
- Common mistakes to avoid
- Progression tips
- YouTube/article recommendations

**💡 Pro Tip:** The AI reads your workout history, so it can give personalized advice like "Your volume dropped 20% last week, that might be why."

### Clarifying Questions

Sometimes the AI needs more info:

```
AI: "I need a few details to design your program:
1. How many days per week can you train?
2. Do you have access to barbells and dumbbells?
3. What's your primary goal?"
```

**Just answer naturally** - the AI understands conversational responses.

### Quick Suggestions

Use the **suggestion buttons** to ask common questions:
- "Create a workout split"
- "Why is my lift stalling?"
- "Improve my form"
- "What muscles does X work?"

## Reading Your Analytics

**Go to:** Analytics (in main menu)

**⚠️ Needs Data:** Most analytics require 2-3 completed workouts

### Time Range Selector

**Top right buttons:** 7D, 30D, 90D, 1Y

Switch between:
- **7 days** - This week's training
- **30 days** - Monthly view
- **90 days** - Quarter view (best for seeing trends)
- **1 year** - Full year progress

### Volume Over Time

**What it shows:** Total weight lifted per day

**How to read it:**
- **Upward trend** = You're getting stronger
- **Spikes** = High volume days
- **Dips** = Rest days or deloads

**Example:**
```
Monday: 15,000 lbs (leg day)
Tuesday: 0 lbs (rest)
Wednesday: 12,000 lbs (upper body)
```

**Use it to:** Track consistency and spot overtraining

### Exercise Progression

**What it shows:** Your performance on specific exercises over time

**Select an exercise** from the dropdown to see:
- **Max Weight** (heaviest weight used)
- **Est. 1RM** (estimated one-rep max)
- **Volume** (total weight × reps)

**How to read it:**
- **All three lines going up** = Perfect progression ✅
- **Flat lines** = Plateau (see plateau detection)
- **Declining** = Consider a deload

**💡 Pro Tip:** Focus on the "Est. 1RM" line—it accounts for both weight AND reps.

### Recent PRs

**What it shows:** Your last 10 personal records

**Types of PRs:**
- **Max Weight** - Heaviest weight you've lifted
- **Volume PR** - Most total volume in one set

**Example:**
```
Bench Press
225 lbs × 5 reps - Max Weight
January 20, 2026
```

**Use it to:** Celebrate wins and track milestones

### Plateau Detection

**What it shows:** Exercises where you haven't improved in 3+ sessions

**How it works:**
- Analyzes your last 3-5 sessions per exercise
- Flags exercises with <2.5% improvement
- Shows first and last 1RM in the plateau

**Example:**
```
⚠️ Plateau: Squat
Sessions: 4
Improvement: 1.2%
First 1RM: 315 lbs
Last 1RM: 319 lbs
Period: Dec 15 - Jan 10
```

**What to do:**
- Change rep ranges (8-12 → 5-8 for strength)
- Add frequency (train it 3x/week instead of 2x)
- Deload for a week, then return
- Ask AI Coach for advice

## Using Auto-Progression

**⚠️ Needs Data:** Requires 2-3 previous sessions with the same exercise

**Where:** During your workout, before the first set of each exercise

### What You'll See

**Suggestion Card** with:
- **Status:** Ready to Progress / Build Volume / Maintain / Deload
- **Suggested Weight:** What to lift today
- **Target Reps:** What to aim for
- **Confidence:** How sure the AI is (0-100%)
- **Reasoning:** Why this suggestion

**Example: Ready to Progress**
```
✅ Ready to Progress!
"You hit 12+ reps on all sets. Time to add weight!"

Suggested Weight: 230 lbs
Target Reps: 8-12
Confidence: 85%

Last session: 225 lbs × 11 reps (Est. 1RM: 300 lbs)
```

### Four Types of Suggestions

#### 1. Increase Weight (Green)
**When:** You've mastered the current weight
**Do:** Add 2.5-5 lbs (upper body) or 5-10 lbs (lower body)
**Confidence:** Usually 80-90%

#### 2. Increase Reps (Blue)
**When:** You're in the target range but not at the top
**Do:** Aim for 1-2 more reps per set
**Confidence:** Usually 70-80%

#### 3. Maintain (Gray)
**When:** You're progressing steadily
**Do:** Keep doing what you're doing
**Confidence:** Usually 60-70%

#### 4. Deload (Yellow)
**When:** Performance dropped >5%
**Do:** Reduce weight by 10% for one week
**Confidence:** Usually 75-85%

### How to Use It

**1. Read the suggestion** before your first set

**2. Decide:**
- **Follow it** (recommended 90% of the time)
- **Adjust slightly** based on how you feel
- **Ignore it** if you have a good reason

**3. Log your sets** as normal

**4. The system learns** and improves next time

### Progression Badges & Streaks

**After logging a set**, you might see:

**Improvement Badge (Green)**
```
+3% ↗️
```
Means you did better than last time!

**Decline Badge (Red)**
```
-5% ↘️
```
Means performance dropped (might need rest)

**Maintained Badge (Yellow)**
```
Maintained
```
Same as last time (totally fine)

**Streak Counter (Blue)**
```
⚡ 5 streak
```
You've improved 5 sessions in a row! Keep going!

### When It Doesn't Work

**No suggestion showing?**
- Need 2-3 previous sessions with that exercise
- Make sure you're logging working sets (not warmups)

**Suggestion seems wrong?**
- Use your judgment—you know your body
- The AI gets better with more data
- Ask AI Coach if unsure

**💡 Pro Tip:** Trust the system when it says "deload." That -10% week will make you stronger long-term.

## Recovery & Fatigue Monitoring

**⚠️ Needs Data:** Requires 7-14 days of training history

**Where:** Analytics → Recovery Status (top section)

### Understanding Your Readiness Score

**Score: 0-100**

| Score | Status | What It Means |
|-------|--------|---------------|
| 85-100 | **Excellent** 🟢 | Well-rested, push hard today |
| 70-84 | **Good** 🔵 | Train normally |
| 50-69 | **Moderate** 🟡 | Watch volume increases |
| 30-49 | **Poor** 🟠 | Consider a deload |
| 0-29 | **Critical** 🔴 | Mandatory rest |

### What Affects Your Score

**Increases Score (+):**
- 2-3 rest days
- Consistent training (not erratic)
- Recent PRs
- Moderate volume

**Decreases Score (-):**
- Volume spike >50%
- Training 6+ days/week
- No rest days
- No recent PRs despite training

### Fatigue Level

**Visual bar showing:**
- **Low** (green) - Normal training
- **Moderate** (yellow) - Be cautious
- **High** (orange) - Reduce volume
- **Critical** (red) - Rest immediately

### Recommendations

The app gives you **actionable advice**:

**Good Recovery Examples:**
- "Well-rested. Good time for hard training."
- "Optimal rest period. Ready for intense training."
- "3 PRs this week! Training stimulus is working."

**Poor Recovery Examples:**
- "⚠️ Consider a deload week (50-60% volume)"
- "Volume increased significantly. Monitor recovery."
- "High training frequency. Consider rest days."
- "No PRs in 2 weeks despite consistent training."

### How to Use Recovery Data

**Before Planning Your Week:**

**Score 70+**
- Train as planned
- Can push intensity
- Add volume if desired

**Score 50-69**
- Reduce volume by 10-20%
- Avoid max effort lifts
- Extra rest day recommended

**Score 30-49**
- **Deload week needed**
- Cut volume by 40-50%
- Keep weights moderate
- Focus on technique

**Score <30**
- **Rest days only**
- Light cardio or mobility work
- No heavy lifting
- Recovery is training

### Metrics Breakdown

**Workouts (7d):** How many sessions this week

**Volume Change:** Current week vs. previous week
- +15% or less = Good progression
- +25% to +50% = Caution
- +50%+ = High injury risk

**Days Since Last:** Rest days since your last workout
- 0-1 days = Might be tired
- 2-3 days = Optimal
- 4+ days = Detraining possible

**PRs This Week:** Recent personal records
- 2-3 PRs = Great recovery
- 0 PRs for 2+ weeks = Check fatigue

**💡 Pro Tip:** If your score is dropping for 2+ weeks, take a full deload week. You'll come back stronger.

## Pro Tips & Best Practices

### Getting Started Right

**Week 1-2: Build Habits**
- Log every workout
- Use warmup toggle correctly (important for PRs!)
- Set realistic target reps (8-12 for most exercises)

**Week 3-4: Gather Data**
- The AI starts learning your patterns
- Auto-progression activates
- Analytics become useful

**Week 5+: Full Intelligence**
- Recovery scores are accurate
- Progression suggestions are confident
- Plateau detection works

### Workout Logging Best Practices

**✅ Do:**
- Log warmup sets as warmups
- Be honest with rep counts (partial reps don't count)
- Use the rest timer (consistency matters)
- Finish the workout in the app (don't abandon it)

**❌ Don't:**
- Skip logging sets (breaks progression tracking)
- Log warmups as working sets (skews PR detection)
- Edit your history excessively (data integrity matters)

### Template Design Tips

**Compound Movements First**
```
1. Squat (compound)
2. Romanian Deadlift (compound)
3. Leg Press (accessory)
4. Leg Curl (isolation)
5. Calf Raise (isolation)
```

**Balance Push/Pull**
- For every horizontal push, do a horizontal pull
- For every vertical push, do a vertical pull
- Prevents imbalances and injury

**Progressive Overload in Templates**
- Use realistic rep ranges (8-12 is most common)
- Don't program too many sets (3-4 per exercise)
- Allow adequate rest (90-120s for compounds)

### Using AI Coach Effectively

**Be Specific:**
❌ "Give me a workout"
✅ "Create a 4-day upper/lower split for hypertrophy, intermediate level, 60 minutes per session"

**Ask Follow-ups:**
```
You: "Create a leg day"
AI: [generates template]
You: "Replace leg press with hack squat"
AI: [adjusts template]
```

**Reference Your Data:**
"My squat hasn't improved in 3 weeks, what should I change?"
(The AI can see your plateau!)

### Progression Strategies

**Linear Progression (Beginners)**
- Add weight every session or every other session
- Small jumps: 2.5-5 lbs upper, 5-10 lbs lower
- Follow auto-progression suggestions

**Double Progression (Intermediate)**
- Increase reps until you hit top of range
- Then add weight and drop back to bottom of range
- Example: 8 reps → 9 reps → 10 reps → add weight, back to 8

**Periodization (Advanced)**
- 4 weeks accumulation (build volume)
- 4 weeks intensification (increase weight)
- 1 week deload
- Repeat

**The app supports all three!** Just follow the auto-progression suggestions.

### Recovery Optimization

**Weekly Structure:**
```
Monday: Upper Body (Heavy)
Tuesday: Rest
Wednesday: Lower Body (Heavy)
Thursday: Rest or light cardio
Friday: Upper Body (Volume)
Saturday: Lower Body (Volume)
Sunday: Rest
```

**Deload Weeks:**
- Every 4-6 weeks
- Or when recovery score drops below 50
- Cut volume by 40-50%
- Maintain intensity (same weights, fewer sets)

**Signs You Need Rest:**
- Recovery score <50 for 3+ days
- Multiple declining performance badges
- No PRs in 2+ weeks despite hard training
- Sleep quality drops
- Motivation tanks

### Analytics Interpretation

**Good Trends:**
- Volume increasing 5-10% per month
- PRs every 2-3 weeks
- 1RM lines trending up
- Recovery score 70-85

**Warning Signs:**
- Volume spikes >30% in one week
- No PRs for a month
- Multiple plateaus
- Recovery score <50 consistently

**What to Do:**
- Ask AI Coach about plateaus
- Follow deload recommendations
- Adjust training frequency
- Check your nutrition and sleep (outside app)

## Common Questions

### "Why isn't the AI suggesting anything?"
Need 2-3 previous sessions with that exercise. Keep logging!

### "The suggested weight seems too light"
The AI is conservative. If you feel strong, add 2.5-5 lbs more.

### "My recovery score is always 70"
You're consistent (good!). Score varies more with 4+ workouts/week.

### "I got a PR but no fireworks?"
Make sure the set wasn't marked as warmup. Also, it must beat your previous best.

### "Can I use this for powerlifting?"
Yes! Just set experience to Advanced and focus on 1-6 rep ranges.

### "Can I use this for bodybuilding?"
Absolutely! Use 8-15 rep ranges and track volume.

### "Should I always follow auto-progression?"
90% yes. Use your judgment if you're sick or very fatigued.

### "How accurate is the 1RM estimate?"
±5% for reps 1-10. Less accurate above 10 reps.

### "Can I track cardio?"
Not yet, but coming soon!
